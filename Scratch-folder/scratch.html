Homemade Pupusas: A Traditional Salvadorean Dish

How To Make Pupusas Recipe



Ingredients Needed

  • 1 cup of Maseca
  • 1 block of queso fresco
  • 1 1/2 cups of warm water
  • 2 cups of mozarella cheese
  • 1 cup of crema salvadorena
  • Optional: Valentina or any other hot sauce

Steps

  1. Mix together in a bowl 1 & 1/2 WARM (not hot, not cold, but warm) water and 1 cup of maseca until slightly sticky or until it doesn't stick to your hands.
  2. Cover it with a clean hand towel for about 2 hours
  3. Mix together the mozarella cheese, crema salvadorena, and queso fresco in a separate bowl. Continue adding according to taste.
  4. Grab a handful of masa (also known as the dough) and flatten it in between the palms of your hands gently and rotate the masa evenly.
    • Side Note: If it gets stuck to your hands, have a bowl of water on the side to wash your hands.
  5. Once the masa is flattened as thin as you can make it, put a chunk of the cheese mix the middle of the dough and wrap the masa around the cheese completely.
    • Side Note: if there are little cracks in the masa, don't worry, that's what makes it yummy! If there are large holes, patch it with extra masa.
  6. Create a little ball with the masa, and then flatten it again using your palms. Gently flatten it rotating it in your palms.
  7. Put the pupusa onto a non-stick skillet that is on medium high heat.
    • If it's not a non-stick skillet, put a light layer of oil on both sides of the pupusa using two fingers and then put it on the pan.
  8. Leave on the pan for about 30 seconds-1 minute, or until browned.
  9. Remove from pan and eat fresh with some salsa, crema salvadorena, or valentina!

Enjoy the deliciousness!!! :)
